Sustainable Desalination

Executive Summary

The core mission of this venture is to establish a profitable, viable, modular, and sustainable desalination enterprise in Turkey, beginning with a pilot project in Izmir. The startup will be built on the principles of environmental responsibility, economic viability, and technological innovation.

Strategic Pillars

Technology

Reverse Osmosis with Energy Recovery Devices for maximum efficiency

Energy

Hybrid solar-wind system for reliable, sustainable power

Partnerships

Collaboration with local university and government

The pilot project will serve as a modular demonstration of feasibility, designed with a low initial budget and utilizing containerized components for rapid deployment. This approach provides a clear pathway for future capacity expansion as market demand grows.

Market Analysis and Opportunity Assessment

"Authorities have been forced to restrict drinking water access to just 10 hours a day, with plans to further reduce this to a mere six hours in the city of Izmir itself."

The Water Crisis in Izmir

The strategic impetus for establishing a renewable energy-powered desalination startup in Izmir is underscored by a severe and escalating water crisis. The primary dam supplying the area has seen its water level plummet to a staggering three percent of its total capacity [534].

Current Challenges

  • • Water rationing: 10 hours/day in Cesme, 6 hours/day planned for Izmir
  • • Groundwater depletion: Wells now require drilling to 170m vs. 8-9m historically
  • • 88% of Turkey's territory at risk of desertification

Tourism Impact

  • • Tourists consume 2-3x more water than local residents
  • • Small hotel occupancy dropped from fully booked to 80% empty
  • • Tourism industry identifies desalination as primary solution

Technological Strategy: Leveraging Proven Solutions

Core Desalination Technology: Reverse Osmosis

Reverse Osmosis has become the global standard for seawater desalination due to its superior energy efficiency and technological maturity. Modern RO systems achieve specific energy consumption of 3 to 6 kWh per cubic meter [836], significantly lower than thermal desalination methods.

System Components

Pre-treatment

Ultrafiltration membranes, chemical dosing for coagulation

RO Membranes

Two-stage configuration for 45-50% recovery rate

Post-treatment

Remineralization, pH adjustment, disinfection

Renewable Energy Integration

Solar PV (Primary)

  • • LCOE: ~0.045 USD/kWh in Mediterranean climate
  • • Modular and easily expandable
  • • Excellent solar resource in Izmir
  • • PV-RO LCOW: 1.24 USD/m³ (Northern Cyprus study)

Wind Power (Complementary)

  • • Generation peaks during night/winter months
  • • Electricity cost as low as 2.68 US cents/kWh
  • • Reduces grid dependency by up to 31%
  • • Complementary to solar generation profile

System Modularity and Scalability

The pilot project will utilize containerized or skid-mounted solutions for rapid deployment and easy scalability. This "building block" approach allows incremental capacity expansion as demand grows.

Containerized Benefits
  • • Factory assembly and testing
  • • Reduced on-site construction time
  • • Easy transport and relocation
Scalability Strategy
  • • "Numbering up" identical units
  • • Match capacity to market demand
  • • Low-risk expansion pathway

Challenges, Risks, and Mitigation Strategies

Technical Risks

Renewable Energy Intermittency

Solar and wind power variability can disrupt continuous operation

Mitigation: Hybrid system design, grid backup, flexible operation

Membrane Fouling

Seawater impurities reduce efficiency and increase maintenance

Mitigation: Robust pre-treatment, proactive maintenance schedule

Financial Risks

High Initial Investment

Significant upfront capital required for technology deployment

Mitigation: Modular pilot approach, grant funding, impact investment

Price Competition

Competing with subsidized municipal water prices

Mitigation: Target premium markets, emphasize reliability value

Regulatory Risks

Grid Connection Policies

Complex regulations for renewable energy integration

Mitigation: Early engagement with TEİAŞ, policy advocacy

Environmental Permitting

Lengthy approval processes for brine discharge

Mitigation: Comprehensive EIA, community engagement
